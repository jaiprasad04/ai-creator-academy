# Sound Design Checklist

Use this checklist during post-production to ensure your AI film sounds as cinematic as it looks.

---

## 1. Dialogue / Narration Layer
- [ ] **Clean Dialogue:** Ensure the generated voice (TTS/Voice Clone) is free of digital artifacts or slurring.
- [ ] **Room Tone:** Add a very quiet ambient layer matching the environment under the dry voice clip so the speaker doesn't sound like they are in a vacuum.
- [ ] **Pacing & Pauses:** Add artificial pauses (0.5 to 1.5 seconds) between sentences. Real people breathe and pause; AI TTS often runs sentences together.

## 2. Ambient Soundscape (Background)
- [ ] **Primary Environment Ambience:** Layer continuous background sound matching the location (e.g., wind rustle for forest, low rumble for spaceship interior, city traffic chatter).
- [ ] **Transitions:** Fade ambience in/out during scene changes to smooth out cuts.

## 3. Foley & Sound Effects (SFX)
- [ ] **Action Syncing:** Add synchronized sound effects for physical actions shown on screen (e.g., doors opening, footsteps, coffee cups clinking, keyboard clicks).
- [ ] **Audio Sweeps:** Use riser or whoosh sounds to match rapid camera pans or transitions.

## 4. Music & Score
- [ ] **Tone Alignment:** Match the music genre to the film's genre.
- [ ] **Dynamic Level:** Duck the music volume by 6dB to 12dB whenever dialogue is playing.
- [ ] **Key Syncing:** Sync visual cuts to the beat or transition points in the music track where possible.

## 5. Master Output Check
- [ ] **Level Balance:** Dialogue should be clearly audible, music secondary, SFX tertiary.
- [ ] **No Clipping:** Ensure the audio master meter never peaks into the red (above 0dB).
