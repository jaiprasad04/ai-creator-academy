## Filled example (skincare-serum category, from Module 5's Worked Example)

**Ad category/product type:** Skincare serum
**How long has it been running (longevity signal):** 47 days, 6 near-identical variants — strong signal it's a kept winner

| Layer | What was done | Why it might work |
|---|---|---|
| Hook | "I stopped using retinol after this happened to my skin." | Scare/curiosity hook that withholds the product name — earns a watch-through before pitching anything |
| Pitch/Problem | Names the specific complaint (retinol irritation) before introducing the product | Sounds like a real complaint, not ad copy |
| Proof/Demo | Before/after skin close-up, timestamped "day 1 / day 14" | A dated, specific claim reads as checkable, not vague |
| CTA | "Link in bio, 20% off first order" | Discount-anchored, not just "shop now" |
| Avatar/Voice tone | Calm, first-person, slightly vulnerable | Matches a skincare-complaint narrative, not a hype tone |
| Video structure | Mostly talking-head, one b-roll cut for the before/after | Keeps focus on the face/proof, not overly produced |
| Captions style | Bold-highlighted keywords, tightly synced | Standard for sound-off viewing |

**Structural takeaway I could reuse for a different product:** A pain-first hook that withholds the product name for the first few seconds, paired with a dated before/after, works well for skincare-category ads — the curiosity gap is what earns the watch-through.
